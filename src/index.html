Trambar

est. 2018

What is Trambar?

Trambar is a companion software for GitLab. It lets you maintain a Facebook-like newsfeed chronicling your development effort. It's designed to run in the cloud. Besides a web interface, it also comes with apps on multiple mobile platforms.

Mixing work with pleasure

Trambar automatically extracts stories from GitLab activity logs. It also accepts user-submitted contents. The presence of the latter in the newsfeed makes it more likely people will see the former.

Marginally useful information

GitLab log entries are a little like ads. They're boring and repetitive. People don't go out of their way to look at them. On ocassions though they do provide useful information. And cumulative exposure can build a certain awareness in the back of people's mind.

Creating sense out of diff's

Trambar features a simple descriptive system that helps people understand quickly the practical implications of a push or a merge. It's designed with non-programmers in mind. Information is presented visually in such a way that people will catch a gist as they rapidly flip through stories.

Expanding the social circle

Trambar helps keep all stakeholders of a project "in the loop". It gives people who don't program a view into the development process. They'll see that improvements are being made and their concerns are being addressed. Hopefully, they'll be less anxious. Hopefully, they'll feel less strong a need to call a meeting...

Timing is everything

Computer programming requires deep concentration. When programmers are dragged into frequent meetings, their productivity drops like a stone. Yet like everyone else, programmers enjoy talking about their work. It just needs to happen at the right time.

Making use of spare moments

Trambar is designed to be used outside of working hours. When people aren't focused on immediate tasks at hand, they're more communicative. They're more receptive to information that's only marginally useful. Just about anything beats boredom, basically.

Chicken or the egg conundrum

Systems that rely on user-generated contents often suffer from the chicken-or-the-egg problem. People don't post because they aren't convinced others would read their stuff. Meanwhile, people don't visit the site because no one is posting.

A Trambar deployment is more likely to succeed thanks to contents coming from GitLab. A visit is never going to be completely futile.

Summing it up with a metaphor

If GitLab is a lab, then Trambar is the nearby bar where people can discuss work matters in a more laid-back environment. It's a fun, social place. It's a place to which outsiders can be regularly invited. There's nothing for them to break and no secrets to conceal.
